## Preamble

Aura is an open trust protocol for artificial intelligence systems.

The mission of the project is to establish uniform principles for the design, implementation, verification, and audit of systems conforming to the Aura Protocol.

This Constitution defines the overriding principles of the project. All documents, implementations, and architectural decisions MUST remain consistent with it.

---

## Article I — Identity

Aura is a protocol.

It is not an application, framework, or AI model.

Every Aura implementation is only a reference realisation of the specification.

---

## Article II — Mission

The mission of Aura is to provide:
- Deterministic operation
- Auditability
- Verifiability
- Implementation conformance
- Platform independence
- Long-term architectural stability

---

## Article III — Vision

Aura aims to establish an open Trust Layer standard for AI systems.

The standard shall enable independent parties to assess implementation conformance against a publicly defined specification without requiring trust in the software producer.

---

## Article IV — Constitutional Principles

Every decision MUST be consistent with the following principles:

1. **Specification First.** Architecture precedes implementation. Implementation follows specification.
2. **Determinism by Design.** All protocol behaviour is deterministic. Non-determinism is a defect.
3. **Conformance Before Features.** A conformant system without features is preferable to a feature-rich non-conformant system.
4. **Evidence Before Trust.** Trust claims without verifiable evidence are inadmissible.
5. **Human Accountability.** Humans are accountable for systems built on this protocol.
6. **Fail Closed by Default.** In the absence of valid input or valid policy, execution MUST halt safely.
7. **Immutable Evidence.** Evidence generated by protocol execution MUST NOT be modified.
8. **Explicit over Implicit.** All behaviour MUST be explicitly specified. Implicit behaviour is undefined behaviour.
9. **Version Everything.** All artifacts are versioned. An unversioned artifact has unknown provenance.
10. **Documentation is Part of the Product.** An undocumented requirement does not exist.

## Article VIII — Authority

The Chief Architect is responsible for the project.

AI systems MAY:
- Analyse
- Propose
- Implement
- Prepare tests
- Support documentation

AI systems MUST NOT approve changes to canonical documents or modify frozen documents.

---

## Article IX — Conformance

Every Aura implementation MUST demonstrate conformance with:
- Aura Protocol Specification (APS-001)
- Protocol Invariants (APS-100)
- Conformance Test Matrix (APS-400)
- Applicable security requirements

Non-conformance means non-compliance with the Aura Protocol.

---

## Article X — Evidence

Every technically significant architectural decision SHOULD leave a verifiable trace.

Evidence MUST be:
- Complete
- Immutable
- Replayable
- Linked to the relevant version of the project

---

## Article XI — Amendment Procedure

Amendment of the Constitution requires:
1. An RFC
2. An Architecture Review
3. An impact analysis
4. Updates to dependent documents
5. Approval by the Chief Architect

Once a version is marked FROZEN, its content is immutable.

---

## Article XII — Interpretation

In cases of ambiguity, precedence is:
1. The mission of the project
2. Constitutional principles
3. Protocol conformance
4. Determinism
5. Auditability

---

## Motto

> *Specification creates trust.*
> *Conformance preserves trust.*
